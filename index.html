Permaculture

Permaculture is a design theory and practice that imitates natural patterns and interactions to produce self-sufficient,sustainable systems. Its main goal is to balance human activities with the environment in order to replenish natural ecosystems and provide necessities like food,housing,and energy. Using renewable resources, increasing diversity, and reducing waste are important.Thus, permaculture createssystems that are long-lasting and ecologically balanced by combining practices like organic farming, water conservation,renewable energy, and community resilience.

In this course, farmers will learn the system of Permaculture. For the price of $17.99, farmers will be able to gain a certificate in their abilities to balance their environment. After learning how to combine your practices, you will be able to make your farm as efficient as possible in the most environmentally friendly way.

Biodynamic Agriculture

Biodynamic Agriculture involves integrating crop farming and livestock farming into one shared system. Biodynamic farms emphasize the relationship between all the different parts of the farm to create an environment more similar to natural growth. Soil on biodynamic farms is healthier because there is less focus on the efficiency of crop growth, and more on the future sustainability of the farm. This method will help prevent major polluting issues like runoff and prevent the creation of non-arable land.

For only $19.50, farmers can learn the processes of naturally combining crops and livestock into their farms. With the help of biodynamic agriculture, farmers can create viable and naturally sustaining farms that keep the balance of nutrients within the soil and the crops and livestock correct.
